## Material Non-Negotiables

### 1. Material is manufactured, not natural
No wood, leather, paper, fabric, or stone simulation. Reference designed objects — injection molding, machined metal, etched glass.

### 2. Translucency is structural, not decorative
Blur and opacity encode meaning: permanence, hierarchy, depth. Never applied for visual interest.

### 3. Depth must be perceivable without color
Strip hue and saturation. If layering isn't still perceivable through value, shadow, blur, and edge treatment, it's broken.

### 4. Edge treatment is deliberate
Every boundary — whether expressed through value, border, shadow, or space alone — is a considered decision.

### 5. The ground is not void
The field at rest has material character. Empty space has substance.

### 6. Surface finish is consistent within a context
Like Color's temperature, finish is a contextual setting — not a per-element choice.

### 7. Material adapts to the medium
Full apparatus on capable displays. Filled-vs-outlined on constrained displays.

## Quiet Mischief in Material

Material mischief lives in **sensory surprises** — moments where the surface does something unexpected but immediately understood.

- A hover that shifts material (gaining subtle sheen as if polished)
- Depth layer shifting in response to cursor (parallax as material depth)
- Surface finish changing with state (matte becoming satin when pressed)

What it's not: Gratuitous blur animations, glass effects everywhere, texture for visual interest.

## The Tick

*The foundational pulse. The universal rhythm beneath every motion character.*

### The Job

The Tick is the heartbeat of the clockwork — the fundamental unit of temporal rhythm that persists regardless of which motion character is active. It is not a member of the Collection. It is the pulse *running through* every member.

In a mechanical watch, the tick is the escapement releasing one tooth at a time — the regulated beat that controls everything else. Every motion character inherits the Tick; none own it.

### The Character

The Tick is **cadence** — the when of motion, stripped of the how. When does a screen transition happen? When does information appear? When does loading indicator advance? These are Tick decisions — rhythm and timing independent of visual transformation.

The Tick carries identity even when it carries nothing else. A system with a considered tick — a deliberate cadence — feels crafted. A system with a careless or inconsistent tick feels broken, even if every individual animation is beautifully executed. Rhythm is the temporal equivalent of spacing: the structural decision that organizes everything else.

### The Survivalist

The Tick is the motion system's survivalist. On a 128×64 OLED, single-color e-ink, or terminal with no animation — the Tick survives alone. No transforms. No easing curves. No visual transitions. Just *when* things happen.

A screen that holds for 1.5 seconds before advancing feels different from one that holds for 800 milliseconds. A cursor blinking at calm rhythm feels different from one blinking anxiously. Information appearing in a considered sequence feels different from all at once.

The Tick on a constrained display is not degraded motion. It is motion at Dimension 1 — the temporal foundation. Complete in itself.

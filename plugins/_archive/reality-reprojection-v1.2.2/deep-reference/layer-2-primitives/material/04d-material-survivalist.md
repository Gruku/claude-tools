## The Material Survivalist

On a 1-bit OLED, monochrome e-ink, or terminal — material vocabulary is reduced to its minimum. What survives?

**Edge and boundary — the material minimum.**

A filled rectangle versus an outlined rectangle is a material decision. A 1px border has material quality. The distinction between a solid block and a hollow frame is material vocabulary at its most constrained.

### The Survivalist's Toolkit

- **Filled vs. outlined** — Two material states in 1-bit. Filled = solid surface. Outlined = bounded area.
- **Relative position** — Spatial proximity becomes primary expression of depth
- **Border weight** — Heavier border = more material presence. Lighter border = less.

### The Experience

The survivalist material experience should feel like a well-designed schematic or technical drawing — minimal means, maximum clarity, every line earning its place.

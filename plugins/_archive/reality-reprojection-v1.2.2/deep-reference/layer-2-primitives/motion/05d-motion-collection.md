# REALITY REPROJECTION

## Layer 2: Visual Primitives — Motion
### The Collection & The Alarm

**Version:** 0.1
**Date:** 2026-02-12
**Part of:** Motion Primitive

---

## The Collection

*The motion characters available to the clockwork. Each defined by how it channels energy.*

The Collection is to Motion what the Voices are to Typography: a cast of instruments, each with distinct personality and job. The designer reaches for the right one based on the moment.

Most interfaces favor one or two members. The full range is available when the context demands it.

### The Hourglass

*Continuous flow. Mass. Gravity.*

Fluid motion with physical believability. Things have mass, accelerate and decelerate. They *travel* to a destination — you feel the journey.

Overshoot is an Hourglass tool — a slight push past the destination before settling.

**Reach for it when:** Spatial transitions, scroll momentum, content repositioning.

### The Pendulum

*Regulated precision. Crafted rhythm.*

Sequential motion character — metered, rhythmic, exact. Where Hourglass flows continuously, Pendulum swings in measured arcs.

Defined by **regulated rhythm**: elements arriving at discernible intervals, transitions following a tempo the eye can anticipate and trust.

**Reach for it when:** Staggered reveals, loading progressions, coordinated multi-element transitions.

### The Flip Clock

*The compound gesture. Fluid into snap.*

Movement that begins as one quality and transforms into another within a single gesture. A split-flap card falls with gravity and lands with mechanical snap.

Defined by **the gear shift** — moment mid-motion where character transforms. The shift should feel inevitable, as if fluid energy had to resolve into precision.

**Reach for it when:** Complex interactions with multiple phases, opening and populating, transitioning and landing.

### The Bell

*The announcement. Declaration made temporal.*

Impact motion character — the moment something *arrives* with full authority, assuming your attention. Where Alarm interrupts rhythm, Bell *is* the rhythm.

A temporal Declaration: same authority, same self-sufficiency, same refusal to hedge.

The Bell has **impact without urgency**. It is not an emergency. It is an entrance.

**Reach for it when:** Brand moments, hero transitions, title appearances, Declaration-scale moments.

### The Digital Clock

*Pure state change. Commitment.*

Instantaneous motion character. 3 becomes 4. No journey, no narration. Just the next state, committed and certain.

The transition is instantaneous, but *context* can cushion: a subtle color flash, a brief value emphasis.

Communicates precision, certainty, and efficiency.

**Reach for it when:** Binary state changes — toggles, checkboxes, tabs. Data updates in live displays.

---

## The Alarm

*The interrupt. The motion that breaks the established rhythm to demand attention.*

The Alarm stands apart from the Collection. Where Collection members describe *how* motion flows, the Alarm describes a motion that **breaks** that flow — deliberately, urgently, because something requires attention.

Defined by **rhythmic disruption**. Works because it violates the established cadence. In a system where motion is considered, the Alarm is the thing that doesn't follow the beat — and the violation itself is the signal.

Can borrow from any Collection member for execution (shake, flash, pulse), but what makes it an Alarm is the *break in pattern*.

Must respect escalation hierarchy from Color's emphasis. Not every interrupt is maximum urgency. A gentle notification might be a single, soft pulse. A critical alert might shake, flash, and persist.

**What it's not:** A license for aggressive motion. Not "make it bounce to get attention." Earned power from rarity — like Color's full saturation.

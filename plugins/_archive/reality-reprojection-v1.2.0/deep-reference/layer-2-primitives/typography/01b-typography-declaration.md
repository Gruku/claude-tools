## The Declaration

*The name on the building. The word that fills the screen. The title that doesn't ask for attention — it assumes it.*

### The Job

The Declaration is the system's loudest voice. It exists for moments where type is not conveying information — it *is* the information. A product name on a startup screen. A section title that anchors an entire layout. A single word on a poster that carries the whole message. It should be able to stand alone as a visual element, self-sufficient, without supporting text or imagery.

### The Character

Heavy. Present. Authoritative without being aggressive. The Declaration has *mass* — you feel the weight of the letterforms, not just their size. It is modern, clean, and precise, but not sterile. There is style in its engineering — the confidence of something machined well. Think: the name that appears when you start a vehicle's system. It is there for three seconds. It needs no explanation. It *is* the identity.

### Two Modes — The Dial in Action

The Declaration is the first place where the tension dials become typographically real.

#### Mode A — The Standard Declaration

The default. Proportioned generously — not compressed or condensed, but not sprawling. Flat, squared terminals. Sharp edges. Geometric underpinning with enough personality to avoid feeling robotic. The needle sits toward Technical, Confident, Monumental.

**Qualities to select for:** Weight at Black/Heavy feels architectural, not bloated. Individual letters have presence — the 'K' has decisiveness, the 'R' has a confident leg, the 'G' doesn't collapse into softness. Generous spacing at display sizes. No vestigial serifs or ornamental details. Works as three letters. Works as one word. Works as a full title if needed, but that's not its primary habitat.

**Qualities to reject:** Overly condensed faces designed for fitting long headlines into narrow columns. Tight spacing that chokes individual character. Excessive curves or rounded terminals. Fashion-forward faces that prioritize trend over durability. Decorative quirks in the letterforms. Anything that needs context to work — the Declaration must be self-sufficient.

#### Mode B — The Nature Declaration

The alternate dial position. Reserved for projects or contexts that call for a different temperature — more sensorial, more human, more historically rooted. This is the illuminated drop cap at the start of a chapter. The art-deco logotype. The gothic title card.

**Qualities to select for:** Expressive curves. Historical DNA — grotesque, gothic, or art-deco lineage visible but not costuming. Works beautifully as a single oversized letter (the drop cap test). Carries drama through form, not just scale. Has the weight and commitment of Mode A but achieves it through organic means rather than geometric.

**Qualities to reject:** Novelty display faces. Anything that reads as pastiche rather than informed reference. Script or handwritten qualities. Excessive ornamentation that competes with the content.

### The Aspirational Vision

Modes A and B are documented as separate selections, but the design intent is that they represent endpoints on a continuous spectrum. If variable font technology, SDF interpolation, or parametric type design allows it, the ideal Declaration is one axis that morphs between the geometric precision of Mode A and the organic expression of Mode B. A project at 100% A or 100% B — or at 40% toward B, where the flat terminals start to soften and the geometry picks up a hint of art-deco curve. This is not currently a font selection criterion, but it is a north star for the system's evolution.

### Scale and Behavior

The Declaration is comfortable at extremes. It is most at home at display sizes — large enough that the letterforms become shapes, not just text. It has no obligation to work at body sizes. It can be tracked wide for monumental presence or set tight for a wordmark. It is the system's boldest gesture.

At its loudest, it fills the viewport. At its quietest, it is a three-letter abbreviation anchoring a corner of a layout. Even quiet, it doesn't become tentative.

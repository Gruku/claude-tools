## The Gear Train

*How the Collection works as a system. Shifting between motion characters within and across interactions.*

### The Principle

A single interaction can pass through multiple Collection members, transforming character as it progresses. One energy source, multiple expressions.

This is the most reactive dial in the system. Where Typography's mode is set per project and Color's temperature is set per context, the Gear Train shifts *per interaction*, sometimes *within* a single gesture.

### Shifting Principles

**Shifts follow energy, not arbitrary sequence.** A gesture that starts fluid doesn't snap to digital mid-stream without reason. The energy transforms — fluid becomes precise at arrival point, precision unfurls into flow at departure point.

**Downshifting is as important as upshifting.** The system must return to calm after an energetic sequence. Without considered downshifts, the system feels like it doesn't know how to stop.

**Neutral is a gear.** The absence of motion is a valid Gear Train position. Not every interaction requires animation. The system's willingness to be still is itself a motion decision.

## Quiet Mischief in Motion

Motion mischief is **surprise in time**.

**Where it lives:**
- The barely-visible layer — subtle rotation, barely-perceptible overshoot, parallax so subtle it reads as depth
- Reactive surprises — element tilting toward cursor, color shifting with mouse position, card breathing when selected
- Gear Train shifts — satisfying shift where fluid resolves into snap with perfect timing
- Alarm personality — notification arriving with tiny bounce, error indicator pulsing once

**What it's not:** Random animation, hover effects on everything, ambient motion that runs when no one's interacting.

## Motion Non-Negotiables

### 1. Every motion has a reason
No animation because "it looks cool." Every moving element answers: what is this motion communicating that stillness could not?

### 2. The Tick persists at every constraint level
At minimum, the system's temporal identity survives through rhythm and cadence.

### 3. Motion respects the user's agency
Does not trap, delay, or obstruct. User can always interrupt animation.

### 4. Easing is never a default
Every curve is deliberate, physically motivated, not mathematically convenient.

### 5. Reactivity requires the user
System does not perform when unwatched. Ambient motion must earn its place.

### 6. The Gear Train shifts smooth
Transitions between Collection members are felt, not seen.

### 7. The Alarm earns its interruption
Reserved for moments that genuinely require it. Overused Alarm is broken.

### 8. Motion does not fight the medium
Adapts to what the medium can express.

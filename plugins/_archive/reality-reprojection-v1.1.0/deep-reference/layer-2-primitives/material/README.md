## Overview

Material & Vocabulary gives the system **substance**. It answers: *what are these things made of?*

The system uses:
- **Opacity** — How much shows through
- **Blur** — How clearly you see through
- **Surface Finish** — The tactile quality (matte, satin, grain)
- **Edge Treatment** — Boundaries (value, border, shadow, space)
- **Depth Expression** — How layers become visible

## Core Principle: Manufactured, Not Grown

Surfaces feel **precision-made, considered, intentional**. LEGO bricks, machined aluminum, Parker pens, clockwork mechanisms.

**Not** wood grain, leather, paper, natural textures. The system references the physicality of *designed objects*.

---

## Key Properties

| Property | Character | Default |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| **Opacity** | Visibility of what's behind | Slightly translucent |
| **Blur** | Clarity of what's behind | Frosted (not transparent) |
| **Finish** | Tactile quality | Quality matte |
| **Edge** | Boundary treatment | Clean, considered, precise |
| **Depth** | Layer visibility | Combination of above |

---

## Core Rules (Non-Negotiables)

- Material is manufactured, not natural
- Translucency is structural (not decorative)
- Depth must work in monochrome
- Edge treatment is deliberate
- Ground has material (not void)
- Surface finish is consistent per context
- Material adapts to the medium

---

## The Survivalist

On 1-bit displays:
- **Filled vs. outlined** — Two material states
- **Border weight** — Heavy = presence, Light = less
- **Relative position** — Spatial proximity expresses depth

Experience like a well-designed schematic — minimal means, maximum clarity.

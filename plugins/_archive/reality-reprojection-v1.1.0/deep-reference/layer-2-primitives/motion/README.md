## Overview

Motion gives the system **time** — the dimension in which it becomes alive, reactive, and present.

The system uses:
- **The Tick** — Foundational pulse, cadence, rhythm
- **The Collection** — Five motion characters (Hourglass, Pendulum, Flip Clock, Bell, Digital Clock)
- **The Alarm** — Interrupt that breaks rhythm for attention
- **The Gear Train** — How characters shift within interactions
- **The Time Vortex** — User presence and reactivity

## The Five Collection Characters

| Character | Quality | Use |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| **Hourglass** | Fluid, physical, mass | Spatial transitions, scroll |
| **Pendulum** | Metered, rhythmic, crafted | Staggered reveals, loading |
| **Flip Clock** | Compound, fluid→snap | Complex interactions, phases |
| **Bell** | Impact, authority, announcement | Brand moments, declarations |
| **Digital Clock** | Instantaneous, commitment | State changes, toggling |

**The Alarm** — Interrupt that breaks rhythm for attention

---

## Core Principle: Motion is Clockwork

**Engineered precision you stop to admire.** Not physics simulation. Not decorative. Motion as a mechanism where every moving part has purpose.

Function that is simultaneously craft. The machine has a heartbeat. Motion is where you hear it.

---

## Key Rules (Non-Negotiables)

- Every motion has a reason
- The Tick persists at every constraint level
- Motion respects user agency
- Easing is never a default
- Reactivity requires the user
- Gear Train shifts are smooth
- Alarms earn their interruption
- Motion adapts to the medium

---

## The Gear Train

How motion characters shift within interactions:
- Shifts follow energy (not arbitrary)
- Downshifting is as important as upshifting
- Neutral (no motion) is a valid gear position

Most reactive dial in the system — shifts per interaction, sometimes within single gesture.

---

## Quiet Mischief in Motion

**Surprise in time:**
- The barely-visible layer (subtle details, almost invisible but felt)
- Reactive surprises (elements responding to cursor, selection breathing)
- Satisfying Gear Train shifts (fluid resolving to snap perfectly)
- Alarm personality (notification bouncing, error pulsing once)

**Not:** Random animation, hover effects everywhere, ambient motion without purpose.

---

## The Survivalist

The Tick — pure cadence — survives when animation is unavailable:
- Screen holds for 1.5s vs. 800ms (feels different)
- Cursor blinks calmly vs. anxiously (feels different)
- Information appears in sequence vs. all at once (feels different)

The Tick on constrained display is motion at Dimension 1 — complete in itself.

## How to Use This Primitive

### Designing interactive motion
1. Read [Collection](05d-motion-collection.md) — understand the five characters
2. Plan the Gear Train — which character for which interaction phase
3. Check [System Rules](05e-motion-system.md) for non-negotiables

### Building for constrained displays
1. Start with [The Tick](05c-motion-tick.md)
2. Use cadence and rhythm alone
3. Remember: Tick is complete temporal foundation

### Building reactive experiences
1. Read [Time Vortex](05b-motion-vortex.md)
2. Understand reactivity intensity follows dials
3. Remember: Only ambient motion that earns its place
